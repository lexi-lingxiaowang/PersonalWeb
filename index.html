About Lingxiao Wang

​I am currently a Postdoctoral Research Associate of Wisconsin National Data Consortium (WiNDC) in the Department of Agricultrual and Applied Economics at the University of Wisconsin-Madison.

I received a PhD in agricultural economics from University of Kentucky (2021), an MS in economics from Ocean University of China, a BS in economics from Central China Normal University, and a BA in philosophy (minor) from Huazhong University of Science and Technology. In 2016, I visited the National University of Political Studies and Public Administration in Romania as an exchange Master student, and in 2020, I explored my Ph.D. dissertation at Cornell University as a visiting researcher.

My research focuses on how agricultural policy shapes consumer behaviors and agricultural markets in the short term, and long-term individual health outcomes by using causal inference methods and subtle study designs. In my Ph.D. dissertation, I investigate how grocery sales taxes affect food consumption and health outcomes including obesity and diabetes, in addition to the causes of the diverse tax rates. My other research covers tobacco, beer and meat-alternative markets emphasizing how consumers react to the policy and environment changes. After joining WiNDC, I am also interested in the applying computable general equilibrium (CGE) modeling in agricultural policy impact analysis.
