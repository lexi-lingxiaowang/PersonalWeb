Hello and Welcome!

I am Lingxiao Wang, and I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate in the Department of Agricultural Economics at the Teaxas A&M University.

I received a Ph.D. in agricultural economics from the University of Kentucky (2021), an MS in economics from Ocean University of China, a BS in economics from Central China Normal University, and a BA in philosophy (minor) from Huazhong University of Science and Technology. In 2016, I visited the National University of Political Studies and Public Administration in Romania as an exchange Master's student. In 2020, I explored my Ph.D. dissertation at Cornell University as a visiting researcher.

My research focuses on consumer behaviors in response to agricultural markets in the short term and long-term individual health outcomes using scanner data. In my Ph.D. dissertation, I investigate how grocery sales taxes affect food consumption and bodyweight. My other research covers tobacco, beer, milk, energy drink, and meat-alternative markets.
